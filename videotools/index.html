// How it works

Private by design

Most "free" video sites upload your file to their servers. VidLab doesn't — the processing engine downloads to your browser instead.

STEP 01

Drop your file

Drag & drop a video or image straight into the tool. It stays in your browser's memory — no upload bar, because there's no upload.

STEP 02

Tweak the settings

Mark the watermark, pick a quality level, choose a new background — every tool gives you a live preview before processing.

STEP 03

Process on-device

Our on-device processing engine and AI models do the heavy lifting using your own CPU/GPU.

STEP 04

Download instantly

Preview the result, then save it. Close the tab and everything is gone — we never saw your file.

// FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is it really free?
Yes — every tool is free with no accounts, no upload limits from us, and no watermark added to your output. The site is supported by ads.
Are my videos uploaded to a server?
No. All processing happens locally in your browser using our on-device processing engine and AI models. Your files never leave your computer.
How does the watermark remover work?
You draw a rectangle over the logo. The tool uses an interpolation filter that reconstructs that region from the surrounding pixels on every frame, so the logo blends into the video. For busy backgrounds you can switch to blur mode instead.
Can I remove watermarks from any video?
Technically yes, but you should only remove watermarks from content you own or have permission to edit. Removing a watermark from someone else's copyrighted content may violate copyright law.
Why is processing slow on long videos?
Because everything runs on your own device (that's what keeps it private), processing speed depends on your CPU. As a rule of thumb it takes roughly the duration of the video. For long files, trim first or lower the resolution.
What's the maximum file size?
The engine holds the file in browser memory, so ~2 GB is the practical ceiling. Videos under 500 MB work best.
