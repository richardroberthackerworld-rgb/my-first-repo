ClipCut: A Free CapCut-Style Video Editor That Runs Entirely in Your Browser

Desktop editors are powerful but heavy. Mobile editors are convenient but stamp watermarks and push subscriptions. ClipCut sits in the sweet spot: a real multi-track timeline editor that opens in a browser tab, needs no install and no account, and exports your video with no watermark — because everything runs on your own device.

What you get

  • Multi-track timeline — separate tracks for video/images, text, motion graphics and audio. Drag to reorder, drag edges to trim, split at the playhead, duplicate with one key.
  • Framing controls — Fit shows your media at its original shape; Fill crops it to the frame; custom crop sliders cut exactly the edges you want, CapCut-style.
  • Transitions — cross fade, wipes, slides, circle reveals, zoom and blur-warp between any two clips.
  • Animated text — typewriter, neon, wave, bounce, glow-pulse and more, with fonts, outline and background pills.
  • AI human effects — on-device segmentation powers background blur, green screen, neon outline and silhouette looks with no green screen and no upload.
  • Color grading — cinematic presets plus full manual control of brightness, contrast, saturation, temperature, tint and vignette per clip.
  • Canvas presets — 16:9 for YouTube, 9:16 for Reels/Shorts, 1:1 for feeds; quality up to 4K, exported as MP4.

A 60-second first project

  1. Import media — drag photos, clips and a music file into the Media panel.
  2. Build the timeline — click each item to add it at the playhead. Image clips default to 4 seconds; drag their edges to taste.
  3. Add motion — select a photo clip, open Motion, pick Ken Burns In. Static photos instantly feel filmed.
  4. Add a title — Text tab → pick a style → it drops at the playhead. Drag it anywhere on the preview.
  5. Transitions & grade — select a clip, choose a transition into it and a color preset. "Cinematic" is a safe default.
  6. Export — pick your canvas and quality, hit Export, and download your MP4. The file is yours; nothing was uploaded.

Why "runs in your browser" matters

Everything in ClipCut — decoding, compositing, effects, AI, encoding — happens on your computer using web technology. That has three consequences worth caring about: your footage stays private (it never leaves your machine), the editor is free to run (no server farm to pay for, so no forced subscription), and there is no upload wait — a 2 GB file opens as fast as a 20 MB one.

Where it fits next to CapCut and Premiere

ClipCut will not replace a color-managed multicam documentary workflow. It is built for the videos most people actually make: photo slideshows with music, talking-head clips with titles, social cuts in 9:16, quick promos with transitions and a grade. For those, opening a tab beats installing four gigabytes — and pairing it with the Compressor and Watermark Remover covers the whole pipeline, free.
