Free AI Tools Every Indian Student Should Be Using in 2026

June 30, 2026 · 5 min read · by Shirandasu Sandeep

A short, honest list — tools that genuinely help you learn, not just finish homework faster.

For doubts: an AI tutor in your pocket

7Solve answers any academic doubt — typed or photographed — with the final answer, the concepts used, full steps and common mistakes, in English, हिंदी or తెలుగు. Thirty free credits daily means three fully-explained doubts a day, every day, at zero cost. The hint mode deserves special mention: it nudges without revealing, which is how homework should work.

For practice: unlimited fresh papers

7Q turns any topic — or a photo of your own textbook pages — into a complete question paper with an answer key: MCQs, blanks, short and long answers, printable as PDF. Self-testing is the highest-return study habit known to learning science, and this removes its only obstacle: finding fresh questions.

For writing: draft, then improve

AI writing assistants are excellent second steps: write your paragraph first, then ask for corrections with explanations. Used this way your English genuinely improves; used as a first step, it quietly replaces your skills instead of building them.

The ground rules

  • Understand, then copy — never the reverse. If you submit it, you should be able to defend every line.
  • Verify numbers. Good tools show working precisely so you can check it.
  • Fewer tools, deeper habits. One doubt solver + one paper generator + your textbook covers 95% of student needs.

The students winning with AI are not the ones with the most apps — they are the ones with the best loop: learn → test → ask → repeat.
