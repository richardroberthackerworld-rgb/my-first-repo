How AI is Revolutionising Music Production in 2025

July 1, 2026 · 7 min read · by Shirandasu Sandeep

Five years ago, separating a vocal from a mix required a professional studio and thousands of rupees. Today, you can do it free in your browser in under 30 seconds. That's the power of what AI has done to music production.

The Shift from Analogue to AI

Music production has always evolved with technology. The electric guitar changed everything in the 1950s. The synthesiser in the 70s. Digital audio workstations (DAWs) in the 90s made recording accessible to anyone with a computer. Now AI is doing it again — but faster than any previous transition.

The defining feature of this shift is democratisation. Tasks that once required expensive hardware, specialist training, or access to a studio can now be done by anyone with a browser.

AI Tools That Are Already Here

1. Source Separation (Stem Splitting)

Models like Meta's Demucs and Deezer's Spleeter can separate a mixed song into vocals, drums, bass, and other instruments with remarkable accuracy. What took hours of manual EQ and phase manipulation in a studio now takes seconds. 7By.in brings this to the browser — no GPU server needed.

2. AI Mastering

Services like LANDR and iZotope Ozone use AI to automatically master a track — adjusting levels, EQ, compression, and stereo width to match professional references. An engineer's ear trained on millions of masters, available at the click of a button.

3. AI Music Generation

Tools like Suno and Udio can generate full songs — complete with vocals, lyrics, and production — from a text prompt. "A lo-fi hip-hop beat with rainy day vibes" produces a finished track in seconds. The quality has rapidly improved from a novelty to something genuinely usable.

4. Vocal Synthesis and Cloning

AI can now clone a singing voice from just a few minutes of audio and generate new performances in that voice. This raises significant ethical and legal questions, but the technology is here and improving rapidly.

5. Intelligent Mixing Assistants

Plugins like iZotope Neutron use AI to detect the instruments in a mix and suggest starting EQ and compression settings. Rather than starting from zero, producers can work from an AI-informed baseline and refine from there.

What AI Still Can't Do

Despite the rapid progress, AI has clear limitations in music production:

  • Emotional nuance — AI-generated music can be technically correct but often lacks the subtle human imperfections and intentional choices that make music emotionally resonant
  • Creative direction — AI needs a prompt; it doesn't have a vision. The creative direction still comes from humans.
  • Perfect separation — stem separation still leaves artefacts, especially on dense mixes or unusual stereo configurations
  • Understanding context — AI doesn't know what a song is for, who it's for, or why certain choices matter

AI as a Tool, Not a Replacement

The most useful way to think about AI in music production is as a very powerful assistant. It handles the technical heavy lifting — noise removal, mastering, stem separation, tuning — freeing the human producer to focus on the creative decisions that actually matter.

The Browser-First Future

One of the most significant trends is the move to browser-based AI tools. With WebAssembly, Web Audio API, and WebGL, modern browsers can run surprisingly complex AI workloads without a server. This means:

  • No upload = better privacy
  • No server costs = lower prices (or free)
  • Works on any device = truly universal access
