15 English Grammar Mistakes Indian Students Keep Making

July 5, 2026 · 6 min read · by Shirandasu Sandeep

The same handful of grammar slips cost marks from Class 6 to competitive exams. Fix these and your writing instantly upgrades.

Agreement and tense

  1. "One of my friends are…" — one is the subject: "One of my friends is coming."
  2. "He do not know" — third person singular takes does: "He does not know."
  3. "I am knowing the answer" — state verbs (know, believe, own) avoid continuous: "I know the answer."
  4. "Yesterday I go" — time words command the tense: "Yesterday I went."
  5. "Since two years" — since takes a point, for takes a duration: "for two years", "since 2024".

Words that betray

  1. "Myself Ramesh" — introductions are "I am Ramesh."
  2. "Discuss about" — discuss carries its own about: "discuss the plan."
  3. "Return back / revert back" — the back is built in: "return", "revert".
  4. "Cousin brother" — English says just "cousin".
  5. "More better / most best" — comparatives don't stack: "better", "best".

Small words, big marks

  1. its vs it's — it's = it is; possession is its.
  2. their/there/they're — possession/place/they are.
  3. "The honesty is important" — abstract nouns drop the: "Honesty is important."
  4. "Advices / informations" — uncountable: "advice", "information", "pieces of advice".
  5. "Cope up with" — it is simply "cope with".

How to actually fix them

Awareness alone fades. Take one mistake per day, write five correct sentences with it, and get your essays checked — paste a paragraph into 7Solve and ask it to correct and explain every error. Explained corrections stick; silent ones don't.
