What Makes a Good Karaoke Song?

The best karaoke songs share a few qualities:

  • Clear, memorable melody — easy to follow even without a visual guide
  • Distinct vocal phrasing — not buried in dense instrumental arrangements
  • Forgiving pitch — not spanning extreme high or low ranges
  • Wide cultural recognition — songs everyone knows

Best Karaoke Songs for Beginners

Start with songs that have a steady tempo, simple melodies, and don't require much vocal range:

  • Wonderwall — Oasis · Simple chord progression, conversational vocal style
  • Let Her Go — Passenger · Gentle range, clear phrasing
  • Counting Stars — OneRepublic · Strong rhythm, easy to follow
  • Shape of You — Ed Sheeran · Repetitive structure, minimal range
  • Tum Hi Ho — Arijit Singh · Slow tempo, very singable melody

Best Karaoke Songs for Intermediate Singers

Songs with a bit more range, runs, and dynamic variation:

  • Someone Like You — Adele · Wide emotional range, powerful chorus
  • Kal Ho Na Ho — Sonu Nigam · Beautiful phrasing, moderate difficulty
  • Thinking Out Loud — Ed Sheeran · Soulful delivery, smooth transitions
  • Rolling in the Deep — Adele · Energetic, full vocal range
  • Perfect — Ed Sheeran · Romantic, good range workout

Best Karaoke Songs for Advanced Singers

Challenge yourself with technically demanding songs:

  • Bohemian Rhapsody — Queen · Wide range, complex sections
  • Dil Diya Gallan — Atif Aslam · Demanding runs and high notes
  • I Will Always Love You — Whitney Houston · Extreme range and power
  • Chandelier — Sia · High belting, emotional intensity
  • A Thousand Years — Christina Perri · Long phrases, breath control

How to Make Your Own Karaoke Track

  1. Find the MP3 or WAV of the song (from a music service you're subscribed to)
  2. Go to 7by.in/tools/vocal-remover
  3. Drop the file in — it processes in your browser (no upload)
  4. Download the Instrumental track
  5. Play it through any speaker or headphones and sing along

Tips for Practice Sessions

  • Use headphones to clearly hear the backing track while you sing
  • Record yourself to track progress (your phone works great)
  • Start at 80% of normal tempo using a DAW or audio player
  • Warm up your voice for 5 minutes before every session
  • Focus on one section at a time rather than the whole song
