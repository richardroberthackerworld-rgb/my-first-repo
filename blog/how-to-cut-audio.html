How to Cut and Trim Audio Files Without Losing Quality

July 1, 2026 · 5 min read · by Shirandasu Sandeep

Cutting audio is one of the most common editing tasks — trimming silence from the start, extracting a chorus, or clipping a song for a ringtone. Here's how to do it perfectly without re-encoding or losing quality.

Why Quality Matters When Cutting

Many online audio cutters work by re-encoding the audio after cutting — converting to MP3 again and applying lossy compression a second time. This "generation loss" compounds the artefacts each time.

7By.in's Audio Cutter works differently. It operates on raw sample data using the Web Audio API, then exports as lossless WAV. The cut points are precise to the sample level with zero re-encoding.

How to Cut Audio with 7By.in

  1. Go to 7by.in/tools/audio-cutter
  2. Upload your file — MP3, WAV, FLAC, AAC all accepted
  3. See the waveform — your entire track is displayed as a visual waveform
  4. Drag the handles — drag the left handle to set the start point, right handle for the end point
  5. Or type the times — enter exact start/end times in MM:SS format for precision
  6. Preview — listen to just the cut section before downloading
  7. Download — get a lossless WAV of exactly the section you selected

Common Use Cases

Creating a Ringtone

Most ringtones are 20–30 seconds. Find the catchiest part of a song — usually the chorus — and cut just that section. Use the time input boxes for precision to nail exactly the right bars.

Removing Silence from Recordings

Podcasts, voice memos, and recordings often have dead air at the start and end. Set the start handle just before the first word and the end handle just after the last word.

Extracting a Sample

Want just the drum intro from a track? Set your cut points around it precisely and download that section for use in your production.

Clipping for Social Media

Instagram Reels and TikTok require audio clips of specific lengths. Cut your audio to exactly 15, 30, or 60 seconds.

Tips for Perfect Cuts

  • Cut on a beat — audio that starts or ends mid-beat sounds jarring. Zoom in on the waveform and cut at a zero-crossing or beat boundary.
  • Leave a little room — cut a fraction of a second before the sound starts to avoid a clipped attack
  • Use WAV input for the cleanest output, especially if you plan to use the clip in a DAW
  • Preview before downloading — listen all the way through to make sure the cut is in the right place

What If I Need Multiple Cuts from One Song?

If you need several sections from the same song, process each one separately and then use the Song Joiner to merge them back into one file with optional silence gaps between each clip.

Quality tip: Always export as WAV from 7By.in, then convert to MP3 only as the final step. Converting to MP3 mid-workflow introduces unnecessary quality loss.
