CA Foundation: How to Prepare and Pass in Your First Attempt

July 18, 2026 · 8 min read · by Shirandasu Sandeep

CA Foundation rewards disciplined practice far more than raw intelligence. Here is a plan that respects that.

Know the battlefield

Four papers: Accounting, Business Laws, Quantitative Aptitude (maths, stats, logical reasoning), and Business Economics. Papers 1–2 are descriptive; papers 3–4 are MCQs with negative marking. You need 40% per paper and 50% aggregate — which means a strong paper can rescue a weak one, but only if you do not leave any paper hopeless.

Accounting: the marks machine

Accounting is the most practice-sensitive subject in the exam. Journal entries, depreciation, partnership accounts, company accounts — each has a fixed method, and the method only becomes yours after 30–40 solved problems. Solve one full problem set daily, and when a step refuses to make sense, get it explained line by line — 7Solve handles CA Foundation numericals with full working.

Law and Economics: write, don't just read

Descriptive answers need structure: provision → explanation → example. Practise writing five answers a week under time pressure. For Economics, graphs earn marks — practise drawing them fast and labelling them correctly.

Quantitative Aptitude: manage, don't master

Most students cannot master all of QA in one attempt — and you do not need to. Secure the high-frequency chapters (ratios, equations, interest, probability basics, series) and attempt intelligently: with negative marking, a confident 70% attempted beats a reckless 100%.

The 3-month rhythm

  1. Months 1–2: finish the syllabus with daily accounting practice.
  2. Month 3: mock tests every alternate day — generate fresh chapter-wise papers on 7Q, review every error the same day.
  3. Final week: revision notes, formula sheets, and full-length mocks only.
